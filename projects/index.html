Current Projects

Stochastic spread of HLB in Florida

Analyzed and explicated the historical spread of citrus greening disease in Florida via stochastic dispersal mechanisms of varied temporal and spatial scales. Collaborated with domain experts at USDA and FDACS to develop a comprehensive spatially explicit, statewide-scale model. Implemented a robust computationally-efficient geo-spatial simulation platform using a combination of TensorFlow and R.

Spectral conditions in nonconvex problems

Motivated by problems in cancer diagnosis (and more general problems in data science/machine learning), we observe strong computational/empirical evidence for the existence of regimes in which there are provable, exact guarantees for the efficacy of natural spectral estimators as solutions to a class of discrete (nonconvex) optimization problems. This project aims to give theoretical justification and verification of this phenomenon.

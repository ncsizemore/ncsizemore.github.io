Digital Twins of Infant Microbiomes

From a small number of longitudinal microbiome profiles in pre-term infants, used a novel machine learning approach to infer the complex time-varying ecoystem dependencies present. From the inferred structure, generated accurate forecasts of microbial evolution, identified infants at risk of poor cognitive development, and found interpretable, testable hypotheses for the key relationships driving network behavior.

VeRITAS: Algorithmic Lie Detection

Developed artificial intelligence (AI) framework for detecting general deception in structured interviews. Validated (AUC $> 0.95$) in the context of symptom fabrication in mental health (PTSD) assessment, our algorithm employs novel generative AI to infer statistical dependencies inherent in true response patterns, and flags responses which violate these subtle constraints. We show that in our methodology having training in forensic psychiatry, or other relevant mental health experience, is not helpful in deceiving the algorithm. Our tool offers an objective, disease-specific, fast (average evaluation time $\leq 4$ min) approach to detect fake PTSD, and if adopted, can ensure that healthcare resources and disability concessions reach those genuinely in need, while helping to maintain integrity of clinical data. Moreover, the ability to identify and help patients who might be malingering due to other mental health conditions, poverty or socio-economic compulsions can improve general health outcomes in disadvantaged communities.

Robust Detection of AI-Generated Text

Recognizing AI generated text by estimating the entropy rate of the symbol stream over the 26+1 letter alphabet (English). We hypothesize that human generated content has a higher entropy rate. Shannon’s experimental approach suggested that English has an entropy rate of 1 bit per letter; here we show that AI generated content typically has much less. We call this algorithm NERO: Nonparametric Entropy-Rate Oracle.

Stochastic Spread of HLB in Florida

Analyzed and explicated the historical spread of citrus greening disease in Florida via stochastic dispersal mechanisms of varied temporal and spatial scales. Collaborated with domain experts at USDA and FDACS to develop a comprehensive spatially explicit, statewide-scale model. Implemented a robust computationally-efficient geo-spatial simulation platform using a combination of TensorFlow and R.

Spectral estimators for a class of nonconvex problems

Motivated by problems in cancer diagnosis (and more general problems in data science/machine learning), we observe strong computational/empirical evidence for the existence of regimes in which there are provable, exact guarantees for the efficacy of natural spectral estimators as solutions to a class of discrete (nonconvex) optimization problems. Here we aim to give theoretical justification and verification of this phenomenon.
